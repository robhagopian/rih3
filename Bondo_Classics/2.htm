Comments Run # 973, November 29, 2004

Contaminate the Queen Hash

The Queen's River is one of the most pristine and pure rivers in RI. It's nominal headwaters originate in the Dead Swamp, just southwest of Wood's Corner in West Greenwich. But the initial stretches are unreliable and inconstant, low in volume and prone to dry-up in times of low annual rainfall. In fact, the major portion of the volume and the real purity of this river originate further west in West Greenwich, at the sources of the Fisherville Brook. These clear waters are protected along their course by The Big River Project, the Nature Conservancy and the Audubon Society. This careful Land Management is responsible for the clean and delightful waters of the Queen's River as it flows south to the Great Swamp. It would take an ecological catastrophe of no small size to muddy these waters. What better place then, to set loose the foul and noisome crew of hashers from the RIH3, than the sources of the Fisherville Brook, at Hopkin's Hill in West Greenwich. Virgin territory? Perhaps, but it will be mightily deflowered and soiled after this hare gets through with it.

This trail has been carefully prepared for the pleasure of all those newcomers who have been joining us lately. Graded trails, carefully selected and pruned shiggy of all sorts, police escorts (possibly) and handcrafted beers in sterilized and hermetically sealed juice jars are promised. The hare has learned 27 new songs, each with more than twenty verses, for the event. So, put away your Turkey left-overs. Dress like a Queen. Wear your worst shiggy shoes. Buy a birthday present for Bondo and Dr WHO. Or just skip the whole thing. Your hare doesn't care. Fewer people = More beer.

After 0.9 miles, Park on Side of Road. You will pass Mailbox # 170 on the left, and go up a short rise into a White Pine Grove. Park where the pine needles are thickest. If you see a Tree Farm/Sawmill on your R, you have gone 100 yds too far.
