Run #1891
Hash: The Hog Calling Time in RI Hash
When: 05/23/22
Hare: Cracker Jackoff
Hashers: Basket BB; Rusty the Red; DonkeyPunch; Pubic Housing; Just The Tip; PG; Just Pat; Satay Man and yours truly, Oozing SD
Where: Wolf Hill/ Smithfield

The Hash: The hare was feeling a little sad and depressed today. Was it that, despite running with the RIH3 for over 3 years, he was incapable of finding virgin trail and kept returning to his stomping grounds? Was it that he felt he never qualified to be hashit? Was it that he became recently unemployed? No! He noted the last time the hash started here, there were close to 28 hashers, and he could only muster the above repeat offenders motley crew! Was he not worthy? Are his trail not as good as a Donkey trail??

With these very thoughts in mind, he explained to the pack his marks, and sent them northwards out of the parking lot, across the road, towards Stump Pond, then north into the woods of Wolf Hill Preserve. PG was distracted and excited and let out delightful squeals as she came upon a set of RI official flora with its bulbous testicular/male genitalia shaped flowers.

Basket and Just Pat were equally distracted with a note where "Map of Trail" were kept. It Read: "Roses are red Violets are blue I wanna f#@k Hope you do too". Just Pat stayed back and was not heard from for quite awhile.

Trail skirted north to the first check. True trail went right. At this point, the FRBs, Donkey and Rusty, decided they would go it alone, not call ON ON to the pack, so as to make it to the BC first to avail of the Capn Daughter. JTT followed suit, but at the next check, went straight and got lost on private property until he hit the power lines and headed southeast.

The rest of the pack, led by the energetic PG and Basket, navigated another set of checks, and hit the Ken Weber trail. At the junction of said trail and Mowry trail, was another check. Of course, the FRBs didn't mark it. True trail took a left up the hill. Oozing went straight, he was not called back. He blew through a false and kept on Mowry trail, hoping to take the next left and circle upto true trail. Well, that didn't quite work as well. He scrambled up the very rocky Mowry trail(green) up a hill/outlook(which would have made quite a scenic BC). Running into another junction unmarked, he took a left at the pink trail. Eventually coming upon the power lines, he looked up and down and saw nor heard anyone.

Doubling back to the next trail junction, he ran into an X, and running trail backwards, to a check that the pack had been through. A good 20-25 minutes behind the pack at this point, he ran through a few checks, and back to the power line!! Again, looking east hoping to see/hear the pack but didn't surmise that the BC may be at the WW2 memorial. He ran the trail through the WWII memorial trail, but no BC, and back to the power lines!!

Trail went down west on the power lines, then south towards Wolf Hill, and the Weber trail. Eventually, going through some checks, he heard the pack at the quarry. Trail was quite unclear at this point. He could see the pack, they could see him, but there was a great chasm divide between the two. Navigating a trail on the rim of the quarry took him on a cliff above the pack at the BC!! Eventually he made it to the BC, and saw JTT and JP had also made it, as well as the walkers. Beer and snacks consumed, the pack was not in a mood to let Oozing catch his breath, and moved on.

Trail was pretty straight forward from here, heading out west towards the reservoir, and the trail adjacent Mountainside Rd. There were grumblings that the On In trail was too long, and unmarked. Back at the start, DP and PH kindly donated extra beer and snacks to the hare in despair, which brought a tear and a smile to his face. General comments were positive, except for Donkey who once again complained about something perhaps the cost of gas.
Down down for the hare who sang a rendition of "Way down in Nagasaki..,".

JTT asked the hash to wish his mum well (meningioma). Satay shared his similar experience. This led to the sharing of many a medical ailment, including Baskets sharting (or was it breathing?) problem, and Donkey's nasty rash.

Oozing was given the deer hump of a hashit for taking WAAAY too long despite selflessly marking trails and calling the pack ON ON, unlike the FRBs.

The ON IN was at Laura's. The $5 4 sliders(hamburger, pulled pork, BBQ chicken) special just can't be beat. And a very decent beer selection. The hostess giggled at Just Pat and his kilt and called him "a bagpipe". Satay Man covered the hare from the goodness of his heart.

A good time had by all.

On On.
