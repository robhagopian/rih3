Run # 2069 The RIH3's 1969th Hash 7/28/25
Where: Effin's Last Resort, Smithfield, RI
Hare: Basket
Hounds: Shemale, WHO, WIPOS, Rusty, Just Pat, Hairy, PG, Just the Tip, Mr. R Oozing, Hairy, Lost Luggage???
Hashit: Just the Tip

Basket announced #2069 would be his last time setting trail, and by god, it felt like the one. It was the kind of trail that takes something from you and leaves you slightly bitter, mostly wetter, and definitely questioning your life choices. At least the merch from Pissonya and Hairy was on point. In attendance was WHO, WIPOS, Rusty, Just Pat, Lost Luggage, Hairy, PG, JtT, Rogers, and Oozy.

We began in the parking lot of Effin’s where the hare clearly intended to teach us humility via uncertainty. Basket laid flour on Saturday, before the heavens opened up to edit his work. By Monday, the marks had achieved what my college art professor would have called “interpretive minimalism.”

Then came the woods, where flour grew shy, the mud grew bold, and the elevation grew personal. Up, down, and sideways. Roll me over then do it again. Trail led us into not one, but several brook crossings, because why end your misery when you can marinate in it?

But salvation shimmered on the horizon: a whisky check, as radiant as a mirage. There we stood, sputtering and delirious, sipping liquid forgiveness from a plastic cup. Spirits rose temporarily.

Crossing through the Bryant University dorm and dining hall parking lots we found a moment for reflection, what if we were all a little smarter? What if we all had half a mind? Never the less, somewhere around mile three we stopped looking for marks and started following the scent of beer, even the hare needed AllTrails for this part. At the beer check we toasted to all of our primary care and specialty physicians: May the manual prostate exam never be replaced by imaging technology.

So here’s to you, Basket, master of mayhem, rain-summoner, and sadist of the slopes. Your trail was a spiritual experience wrapped in a mild cardiac event. We may have cursed your name between gasps for breath, but we meant every word with love.

On-after at Laura’s provided $8 22oz draft Guinness and $10 slider meals. On-on you magnificent bastard. May your next trail have fewer hills, or at least more whisky.
