Run# 1945 The dead pirate lives again Hash
Date: 6/5/2023
Weather: rain broke and temps warming
Location: East Greenwich Cove Overlook
Hounds: Basket, Pubic, Just Pat, Rusty, Oozy, Satay, WHO, Sex-ray, Amish, Period Shit, Hairy, Tip, SnowBurp
Hare: Wipos
Hashit: Hairy

The hare was so cute setting a picnic table prelube with a vase of flowers and a floral butt plug dildo. Many luxurious beers were shared from various parties.

Trail started in the parking lot above the boat ramp, the first mark (possibly last mark) of flour adorning the door frame of a mysterious portal. Maybe there's better trail on the other side?

Flour marks down the stairs led to a fork, marshes and scrambles to the right, pavement and docks to the left. The whole pack went right. Basket and Hairy went left. Soon though, we heard the 'on back' calls of halfminds enjoying a wee whiskey on the docks.

Trail then circumnavigated the town dump, under the train tracks, and up the hill into the neighborhood. It is here that the hare began marking the backside of telephone poles with mere smudges of flour. Pretty soon there were families hollering at us for spooking their dogs. Just about the time that the pack began to cross the tracks again Basket found a public use rental scooter. He was then to be lost in his phone for an hour or so.

After crossing over the tracks this time we were just about at the cars again, but a few dobs of flour led us into scalloptown park. After circling the peninsula three or four times, and meeting some cute puppers, the hare finally showed us his wood. He decided not to mark the beer stop with flour but instead with sticks...

At the beer check there was a beautiful view of the water, that we couldn't see. There was also a beautiful sunset hidden behind the canopy, in which the entire rhody hash couldn't find the hole. The 4 Captains daughter's were 3 months old. The empty bags of chips were arranged into Hebrew texts. With 2.69 miles of trail down, the pack began the 1 mile asphalt walk back to the cars.

Circling up back at the elegantly adorned picnic table we were given a nostalgic selection of beer, bottles of harpoon and Guinness. We witnessed an East Greenwich ambulance dispose of a body in the town dump. There was much communal yoga. Rating the run the pack came up with too much pavement, too little flour, not Virgin territory. Numerical total: a bad positive 6.9. Hairy got hashit for following basket and he tried to sing an original tune but couldn't remember it.

SexRay was given a rhody name - SexArrow, an homage to the hares's new flour symbol, the Xarrow. May she go forth on the next of life's adventures with a bit of rhody in her.

On-after at McKinleys was decent. We were lucky enough to have $20ish tabs due to 1/2 price pizza and $6 margaritas.
