Another Effin JIMA Hash

Run #1159, June 16, 2008

Hare:  Basket

Location:  Effins Pub, Smithfield

Weather:  80’s, Intermittent Rain, lightning .

Present:   Dr WHO, Microhard, Amish It Head, Async, Bondo Jovi.

The Run:
The RIH3 is an optimistic hash. This is in the face of incontrovertible evidence to the contrary. For example, Justin My Ass has been on the hareline for sometime. He has set "trail" maybe three times, and combining those three experiences into one might actually make one real trail. So it should have been no surprise that Basket called upon himself to take over when JIMA wanked off yet again. (Some might say that that would be small improvement, but the RIH3 is also a charitable hash. At least the start was not the Burrillville Middle School.) So only a few desperate ones showed, along with Microhard (who makes appearances that roughly correspond to the hatching of the 17 year cicadas, it would seem.)

The start was from Effin's parking lot on Rt 5 in Smithfield. The pack was directed to look for marks across the street, and they quickly found trail at the K ofC, or VFW or Elks or whatever, just south of Box Seats. WHO went straight for the path along the reservoir, while Async and Amish found the true trail through the adjacent woods. They came together soon, and continued south. The spillway was crossed, an unecessary excursion as it happened, since trail led back out and across Rt 5 to find Bondo and Ben walking leisurely along. They entered the trail to the river and continued to a check under the 116 bridge.

Async found the false mark further along the river. Amish and WHO considered crossing the river, but seeing no marks, decided against it. Finally they tried right, and came up onto Rt 116 heading southwest. An old, make that ancient, flour arrow was seen, indicating entry into the industrial lot. That flour must have some staying power as the last time the hash led here was Run #1138 in January! But there were no fresh marks that way. Microhard found true trail, entering the woods uphill and south.

The trail was now narrow paths with some bushwhacking. It couldn't really be called shiggy, but it was a pleasant change from the trail thus far. Amish found trail at the next check which turned east to the powerlines. They soon were heading north, and back to the path on the south side of the river. Some confusion ensued and the hare looked amused. He was the only one. Finally, WHO took the hare's hints and turned west, running through the "X" and back to the 116 bridge. Some fresh marks were found. Oh, that tricky hare! Wanker! Sure enough, the river was to be crossed. Bowing to the inevitable, they swam across (except for Bondo, who took the bridge). The beer was recovered from its hiding place by the hare and they had their BC at last.

Trail back was through the woods, with the few checks being ignored by most. Async and WHO bushwhacked straight to the cars, the rest took the slightly longer trail. With some nice rain and lightning starting, they held the circle at the north end of the parking lot, concealed by a hedge and the weather. Ratings for the run: overused territory, no bimbos, and limited shiggy were the main comments. Total: -6.9! Hashit was given to Bondo for avoiding the river crossings. They finished up and moved into Effins, where the pool was closed because of the lightning storm. Another wasted evening for the Rhode Island Hash.
